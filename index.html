📘 DFA Minimization

DFA Minimization is a fundamental concept in Theory of Computation. It reduces the number of states in a DFA while preserving its language.

The goal is to construct a minimal DFA that is equivalent to the original DFA.

🎯 Why Minimization?

  • Reduces complexity
  • Improves performance
  • Eliminates redundant states
  • Used in compilers & pattern matching

⚙️ Myhill–Nerode (Table Filling)

Based on equivalence relation between states.

  • Mark distinguishable state pairs
  • Propagate markings
  • Unmarked → equivalent

⚡ Hopcroft Algorithm

Efficient partition-based minimization algorithm.

  • Split into final/non-final
  • Refine partitions
  • Time complexity: O(n log n)

🧠 Step-by-Step Process

  1. Remove unreachable states
  2. Divide into final & non-final states
  3. Check distinguishability
  4. Merge equivalent states
  5. Construct minimized DFA

Original DFA: 5 states
Minimized DFA: 3 states
Reduction: 40%
